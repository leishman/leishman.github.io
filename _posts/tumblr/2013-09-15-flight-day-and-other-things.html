Well, travel day is finally here and I’m sitting in the airport as I type this. I can’t wait to board the plane and finally get this trip started. First stop, Tokyo!

That said, there are definitely some people I’ll miss over the next two months, but thanks to this beautiful thing that we call the interwebs keeping in touch is as easy as pie.

Maptia

Maptia, a startup with a mission to let people more effectively share their travel stories, just sent me an invitation to be an early tester! I’m really excited, as I’ve been following the founding team’s story quite closely. I first read about Maptia on Quora, where one of the founders wrote about how their team (mostly British) moved to a beach town in Morocco to save money.Â 

They’ve developed a clean, quality product and I can’t wait to use it more thoroughly once I have some content to post from my upcoming trip.

The main purpose of the site is to allow users to share their experiences through text/multimedia and, soon, create their own custom maps of the world. It’s off to a great start and I’m excited to see how it will grow once it opens up to the public.

Personal Projects:

My personal site is at a good place right now. I’m going to re-focus my efforts on Maply. The next step with Maply is to create an elementary version of the US map with basic information for each state. This will be targeted towards kids.

I also plan to put Maply on Rails and create an interface that allows users to toggle the map that they see. There’s so much to do!!
