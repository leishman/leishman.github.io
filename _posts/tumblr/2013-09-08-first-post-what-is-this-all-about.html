Last Friday I quit my job as a management consultant, permanently untethering myself from any regular source of income to begin my climb (or dive?) into the tech world. 

I wish I could say that I’m some software guru who has tech firms slobbering over him. I wish I could say that I’m an entrepreneurial genius who has VC’s lining up to throw money at him. I wish I could say that I have a network of C-suite executives to tap. I wish I could simply say that I have a solid business idea. But the truth is, I’m just a guy with big dreams and not enough sense to think I can’t reach them.

This blog will be an honest account of my personal and technological journey.

I’ve never kept a blog before. I’m a young guy and, frankly, I’m not going to pretend to have any wisdom to impart to you. However, I recognize that my current path is unconventional and I do hope that sharing my successes and failures will be valuable to readers considering pursuing any similar endeavors. At the very least, I hope this turns into an interesting story.

So enough fluff already! Who am I and what the hell am I doing?

Name: Alexander Leishman
Age: 23
Education: University of Maryland, College Park (B.S. Aerospace Engineering 2012)
Occupation: Funemployed


Background:
I spent the past year working for a management consulting firm. I learned a tremendous amount and met some incredible people, but I realized that it just wasn’t the right fit for me.

My real passion is in technology and entrepreneurship, so I decided to take a leap and apply to Dev Bootcamp. For some reason they accepted me and I will be starting the program on January 6th, 2013!

Between now and the start of the program I will spend my time traveling, programming and learning as much as I can.

After Dev Bootcamp I hope to find a job as a developer in the San Francisco area. My real dream, however, is to start a successful company of my own someday and I plan on working my ass off trying to make this happen.
